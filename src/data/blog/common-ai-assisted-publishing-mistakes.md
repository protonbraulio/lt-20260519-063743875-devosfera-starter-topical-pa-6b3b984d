## Mistakes that weaken AI-assisted Publishing

Operations managers in Toronto often face common pitfalls when implementing AI-assisted publishing. These mistakes can hinder the efficiency and effectiveness of the process. Let's explore some of the most frequent issues and how to address them.

One common mistake is not clearly defining the owner of the AI-assisted publishing process. This lack of clarity can lead to confusion, delays, and poor decision-making. To fix this, ensure that there is a designated owner responsible for overseeing the process and making critical decisions.

Another mistake is not identifying the required inputs and expected outcomes. Without a clear understanding of what is needed and what should be achieved, the AI-assisted publishing process can become chaotic and ineffective. To catch and fix this issue early, document the required inputs and expected outcomes for each stage of the process.

Inconsistent decision criteria can also weaken AI-assisted publishing. Different teams may use varying criteria to make decisions, leading to inconsistencies and errors. To address this, establish clear and consistent decision criteria that are understood and followed by all teams involved in the process.

## Why these mistakes keep showing up

These mistakes persist due to a lack of communication, inadequate training, and resistance to change. Operations managers may not be aware of the impact of these issues or may struggle to implement changes due to competing priorities or limited resources.

To catch and fix AI-assisted publishing issues early, operations managers should foster a culture of continuous improvement. Encourage open communication, provide regular training, and involve teams in identifying and addressing process inefficiencies.

Another reason these mistakes keep occurring is the lack of metrics to measure the success of AI-assisted publishing. Without clear metrics, it's difficult to identify areas for improvement. To prevent this, establish key performance indicators (KPIs) that track the progress and effectiveness of the AI-assisted publishing process.

## How to catch and fix AI-assisted Publishing issues early

To catch and fix AI-assisted publishing issues early, operations managers should implement a robust quality control process. This can include regular audits, peer reviews, and automated checks to identify and address potential issues before they become major problems.

Another approach is to involve end-users in the AI-assisted publishing process. By engaging with the people who will ultimately use the published content, operations managers can gain valuable insights into potential issues and areas for improvement. This can help identify and fix issues early in the process.

Additionally, operations managers should establish a clear escalation path for addressing AI-assisted publishing issues. This can include designated points of contact, clear communication channels, and a well-defined process for resolving issues quickly and effectively.

## Checks to repeat after the fix

After fixing AI-assisted publishing issues, operations managers should repeat several checks to ensure that the improvements are sustained and that new issues do not arise. These checks can include regular audits, user feedback, and performance metrics.

One important check is to verify that the AI-assisted publishing process is still aligned with the organization's goals and objectives. Over time, these may change, and the process should be adjusted accordingly to ensure it remains relevant and effective.

Another check is to ensure that the AI-assisted publishing process is still compliant with relevant regulations and standards. This can include data privacy laws, accessibility guidelines, and industry-specific requirements. Regularly reviewing and updating the process can help ensure ongoing compliance.
